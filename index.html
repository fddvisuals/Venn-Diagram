AMERICAN POLICY AIMS TOWARDS TEHRAN’S MISSILES

  • Prevent Iran from developing means to deliver a nuclear weapon
  • Impede Iranian missile production
  • Impede qualitative missile advances Thwart foreign procurement of parts and systems
  • Intercept, expose, and deter Iranian missile and missile-related technology transfers to other actors
  • Deploy missile defenses to offset Iranian missile advances through deterrence by denial
  • Get Iran to address missiles in talks
  • Be able to hold Iran’s missile assets and infrastructure at risk militarily
  • Minimize the political impact of Iran’s missile systems in its coercive diplomacy in the region

IRAN’S MISSILE POLICY AIMS
‍

  • Retain option for delivering a potential nuclear weapon
  • Continue producing missiles and other projectiles
  • Continue drive for more precise, longer-range, and more reliable missile systems
  • Continue illicit foreign procurement
  • Continue missile and missile-related technology transfers to proxies and partners
  • Use missiles to bolster deterrence by punishment
  • Reject including missiles in talks
  • Develop a more mobile, dispersed, and survivable missile force
  • Maximize the impact of missiles on regional coercive diplomacy
